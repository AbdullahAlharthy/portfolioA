Customer Churn Prediction

Built a predictive system to identify telecom customers at risk of churn using CRISP-DM methodology, ensemble models, and synthetic data generation.

PythonLightGBMTabDDPMSMOTETomekH2O AutoMLPyCaretSHAPOptunaScikit-learnPandas
🎯 The Problem
Telecom companies lose significant revenue to customer churn. Acquiring a new customer costs 5× more than retaining an existing one — making early churn prediction a high-value business problem.
⚙️ Our Approach
We applied the full CRISP-DM methodology across 20+ experiments — testing 9 ML models, 6 balancing strategies, 4 encoding schemes, and a novel TabDDPM synthetic data pipeline to handle severe class imbalance.
✅ Key Result
Best model: LightGBM + TabDDPM + SMOTETomek + Threshold Tuning — achieved Recall 0.78 (catches 78% of churners before they leave), with a 7% F1 improvement over the baseline from synthetic data alone.

Business Insights
💸
Retention over acquisition: Identifying at-risk customers before they leave costs 5× less than winning them back — making Recall (0.78) the metric that directly maps to revenue saved.
⚖️
Threshold tuning = cost control: Lowering the classification threshold increases Recall at the cost of Precision — a deliberate trade-off justified when false negatives (missed churners) are more costly than false positives (wasted offers).
🧬
Synthetic data as a business lever: TabDDPM-generated samples improved F1 by 7% — demonstrating that data augmentation can substitute for expensive real-world data collection when class imbalance limits model performance.
🔍
SHAP for trust and action: Explainability via SHAP converts black-box predictions into actionable signals — e.g., identifying that month-to-month contracts and high monthly charges are top churn drivers, guiding targeted retention campaigns.

Heart Disease Prediction

Analyzed 2,182 patient records (1,889 after cleaning) using RapidMiner. Three tasks: EDA, Decision Tree classification (12 models compared), and K-Means clustering for patient segmentation.

RapidMinerDecision TreeK-Means ClusteringEDAStatistical Analysis
🎯 The Problem
Heart disease is a leading cause of death globally. Early and accurate diagnosis from clinical data can dramatically improve patient outcomes — but raw data from multiple sources is noisy and inconsistent.
⚙️ Our Approach
We merged 5 public datasets (2,182 raw records) into one clean 1,889-record dataset with 14 clinical features, then ran three analysis tasks in RapidMiner: exploratory analysis, decision tree classification (12 configs), and K-Means patient segmentation.
✅ Key Result
Best decision tree: Information Gain + 3.5% min leaf — achieved 85% accuracy and an overall score of 0.87. K-Means (K=3) revealed clinically meaningful patient clusters segmented by age, thal, and chest pain type.

Credit Card Fraud Detection

Developed ML models to detect fraudulent transactions. Applied class balancing, 5-fold cross-validation, and visualized results with ROC curves, feature importance, and confusion matrix.

PythonRandom ForestDecision TreeLogistic RegressionScikit-learnSeabornMatplotlibPandas
🎯 The Problem
Credit card fraud causes billions in annual losses. Fraud transactions are rare — severe class imbalance means naive models simply predict "not fraud" and still appear accurate, hiding real detection failure.
⚙️ Our Approach
Balanced the dataset to 2,145 samples per class, trained three classifiers with 5-fold cross-validation, and used AUC-ROC (not accuracy) as the primary metric — ensuring the model genuinely distinguishes fraud from legitimate transactions.
✅ Key Result
Random Forest achieved AUC 0.98. The most surprising finding: transaction amount alone drives 87% of the fraud signal — geographic location adds almost zero predictive value in this dataset.

Object Detection in Drone Imagery

Trained YOLOv8 and YOLO11 models on the VisDrone2019 aerial dataset. Doubled training data to 12,942 images via augmentation. Achieved 34.6% mAP with YOLO11l.

YOLO11lYOLOv8mYOLOv8nPyTorchCUDAOpenCVUltralyticsImage ProcessingData Augmentation
🎯 The Problem
Aerial surveillance drones capture crowded, small-object scenes at oblique angles — conditions that break standard detectors. The VisDrone benchmark is one of the hardest object detection datasets due to tiny objects, dense crowds, and wide altitude variation.
⚙️ Our Approach
We ran 4 systematic training experiments — scaling both model size (YOLOv8n → YOLOv8m → YOLO11l) and data volume (6,471 → 12,942 images via custom augmentation) to isolate the impact of each factor on detection accuracy.
✅ Key Result
YOLO11l achieved mAP@50 = 0.346 — cars detected at 77.5% accuracy, real-time speed at 1.9ms per image. Data augmentation alone improved mAP by +6% before any model upgrade.
