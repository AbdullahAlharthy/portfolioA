The Problem

Jeddah's rental market is opaque

Jeddah has no transparent pricing reference for apartments. Listings exist in Arabic and English, prices vary wildly by district, and most people rent without any way to check if the price they're seeing is fair. A coastal apartment in Al-Shati and a budget unit in an inland district can differ by 4× — but renters often have no idea which bracket they're in.

The goal was to build a model that estimates rent from apartment specs alone — and to make the insights usable through an interactive dashboard anyone can explore.

Data & Features

11,818 real listings, 60+ engineered features

I scraped 11,818 real Jeddah rental listings, then spent the majority of the project time on cleaning and feature engineering. Raw listing data is messy: duplicate entries, inconsistent area units, missing values, and Arabic descriptions mixed with English.

The most interesting feature engineering was the NLP embeddings. Listing descriptions in Arabic and English were encoded using a multilingual Sentence Transformer model (768-dimensional embeddings), then compressed to 128 dimensions via PCA. This let the model use the qualitative content of a listing — phrases like "sea view" or "fully furnished" in either language — as a predictive signal.

Other engineered features included: district-level median rent, price-per-sqm by area tier, amenity scoring (pool, gym, parking weighted by their individual price associations), floor-level encoding, and a listing freshness score. All feature transforms were strictly fitted on training data only — no leakage.

Modeling

CatBoost + Optuna HPO, 40 trials

CatBoost was selected as the primary model after baseline comparisons with LightGBM, XGBoost, and Ridge regression. CatBoost's native handling of categorical features (district names, property types) avoided the need for manual encoding of high-cardinality columns, which mattered here.

Hyperparameter optimization used Optuna for 40 trials on a held-out validation set, tuning learning rate, depth, regularization, and iteration count. Final evaluation used 5-fold stratified cross-validation (stratified by district tier) to ensure results were stable across geographic segments of the market.

SHAP values were computed post-training to understand which features drove individual predictions. District location alone accounted for ~16% of model importance — more than area and bedrooms combined. The NLP embeddings contributed ~27% of total importance as a group, confirming that qualitative listing content carries real pricing signal.

Results & Dashboard

R² = 0.758 with a live interactive map

The final model reached R² = 0.758 on test data and CV R² = 0.722 ± 0.006 across 5 folds — a stable result that generalizes across Jeddah's rental market. MAPE of 13.8% means predictions are typically within about SAR 468/month of actual rent across all price levels.

The prediction spread widens above SAR 60k/year — the luxury segment has fewer training samples, so predictions carry more uncertainty there. This is an honest limitation the model can't fully overcome without more high-end listing data.

The interactive dashboard includes a Voronoi district map color-coded by median annual rent (SAR 18k–72k), a live rent estimator where users can input apartment specs, and district comparison charts. The coastal Al-Shati area shows approximately 4× the median rent of inland budget districts.
